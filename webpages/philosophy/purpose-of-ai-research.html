The Importance of AI

Mankind's quest to understand and apply the principles of intelligence is the most significant endeavor of our existence. All great works of man, the blessings of modernity, and the marvels of our species stem from the most important six inches of our lives and the seat of our intelligence, our brain. Since the dawn of our earliest ancestors, human cognitive labor has reaped a bountiful intellectual, technological, and cultural harvest. This crop of the mind includes an inexhaustible list of achievements and wonders: reason, science, engineering, philosophy, agriculture, the free market, democracy, art, music, literature, poetry, drama, fiction, and many other examples.

By using our intelligence, a significant population of mankind has transcended an otherwise vicious life of unnecessary suffering and a propensity for animalistic violence. This is not an attempt to overlook or excuse the cold reality of our ever-present animal nature, the unique malevolence spawned from human cognitive abilities, or the troubles people still face today. In fact, constant knowledge and reminder of these concepts are crucially important and will be discussed on this website. However despite our dark side, the ultimate source of human success and prosperity thus far is due to our intelligence.

Because intelligence thus far has been beneficial to all areas of our lives, the next logical (and perhaps inevitable) step is to build intelligent machines. Throughout history technology has enabled men and women to do more with less: the loom which provided less-expensive higher-quality clothing, the printing press enabling the distribution of books and an education revolution, advances in agriculture and medicine allows for longer lives and less suffering, and many other examples. It is important to note that the rate at which technology has grown is not linear, it is exponential. Moore's law
