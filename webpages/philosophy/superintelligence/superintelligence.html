Superintelligence

"Thus the first ultraintelligent machine is the last invention that man need ever make..." -I. J. Good [1]

Because of this profound success, the next logical (and perhaps inevitable) step is to build intelligent machines. It is important to note that the rate at which technology has grown so far is not linear, but exponential. The less capable yesterday's technology built the more capable today's technology which will build the even more capable tomorrow's technology. Thinking machines build better thinking machines to build better thinking machines....

The technological singularity (also, simply, the singularity) is the hypothesis that the invention of artificial superintelligence will abruptly trigger runaway technological growth, resulting in unfathomable changes to human civilization.
