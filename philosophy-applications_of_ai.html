Applications of AI

What are the potential applications of machine intelligence technology and how may it serve humanity?

Transhuman Sensing

Humans directly experience perceived reality within specific sensory systems: sight, hearing, touch, taste, smell (a more detailed look at the human sensory systems may be found in Neuroscience - Human Overview). However, we know of forces beyond our direct sensory experience like electromagnetism, ultrasound, data flow through the internet, gravity, quantum forces, et al. These indirect sensations require equipment and technology to transform indirect information to direct, human intuitive information like mathematical and graphical models.

A machine cortex may operate like a human cortex: pattern recognition, inference, prediction, creativity, selective attention, etc. However, a machine intelligence is not bound by human biological sensing capabilities. All human wonders of technology, art, and culture have been achieved through these 5 senses supplemented by models. What undreamed of could AI create if they had an intuitive sense of sensory spaces humans labor their whole lives to grasp and understand. Imagine if you could figuratively "see" gravity. Advanced concepts like special relativity would be relatively intuitive, much like seeing your pet dog or cat lick itself. Imagine you could directly access the wireless internet with your brain by sensing the right electromagnetic waves.
